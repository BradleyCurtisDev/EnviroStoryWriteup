# 2309516 - Environmental Storytelling









ADD / Devlog Structure Guide
Evidence should be presented in a single PDF/PowerPoint/HTML/Word document. Students will select
1 Primary Role (Assessment Focus) and 2 Secondary Roles (Supporting contributions). Your submission
must clearly provide evidence of the following across your roles contributions.
1. Exploring (Research & Inquiry)
“Where did your ideas come from?”
Any reference gathering, theory investigation, and initial ideation. Analysis of genre,
style, and mechanics.
• Evidence Examples: Mood boards (PureRef), initial sketches, paper prototypes, flowcharts,
research notes, style guides.
2. Making (Production & Iteration)
“How did you build it?”
The bulk of your production work. Documentation of the workflow from greybox to
final polish. Showing iteration and improvement.
• Evidence Examples: Screengrabs of work-in-progress, wireframes, code snippets, material
graphs, asset breakdowns, before/after comparisons of critiques.
3. Connecting (Collaboration)
“How did you work with the team?”
Reflect and show evidence of Leadership, pivot-point communication and or conflict
resolution. Ensuring your work integrates seamlessly with others.
• Evidence Examples: Meeting minutes, Discord logs, Agile workflows evidence (Trello/Jira cards,
Sprint Reviews), feedback given/received, file naming convention adherence.
4. Situating & Synthesizing (Reflection - Context & Conclusion)
“What is the final result?” and “Why did you make these choices?”
This section should be reflective and show critical thinking in the justification of your
choices, the work you have done in your roles and how it supports the overall project.
Explaining how your work supports environmental storytelling, gameplay, aesthetic
vision, professional practice and optimization.
• Evidence Examples: Before and after screenshots / gifs, Annotations on design choices, narrative
justification, Industry alignment considerations, project timeline considerations, accessibility
considerations etc.
